The Emery’s Light Children’s Book Series

Format: Picture books · Role: Writer, illustrator, emotional development designer
Ages: 0–5 · Style: Soft watercolor, gentle themes, sister-led emotional guidance

Overview

Emery’s Light is a children’s book series designed to help babies, toddlers, and preschoolers understand their feelings through soothing watercolor art and nurturing storytelling. Each book features Emery — a gentle, magical, big-sister guide — who helps Josie make sense of her emotions in a safe, loving, and developmentally appropriate way.

The books are crafted with a calming sensory style, predictable pacing, and simple emotional vocabulary, ideal for bedtime routines, calm-down moments, or early social-emotional education.

Books in the Series

Book 1: What’s That Feeling, Josie?

An Emery’s Light Story

This first book introduces Josie’s emotional world through a gentle, magical encounter with Emery — her “big sister from the stars.” The story validates early feelings, introduces emotional language, and models mindful breathing.

Book 2: Josie Tries Again

An Emery’s Light Story

Book 2 focuses on frustration, perseverance, and the calming power of pause. Josie struggles to stack her blocks — and just as frustration peaks, Emery appears to guide her through the moment with grounding breath and a return to calm.

Art Direction

The Emery’s Light books use a soothing, minimalist watercolor style designed for sensitive young nervous systems. Visual noise is intentionally minimized to help toddlers focus on emotion, gesture, and connection.

  • Soft, rounded character features
  • Warm pastel palettes
  • Gentle gradients with low contrast
  • Minimal background clutter
  • Sparkle motifs signaling emotional presence

  • Consistent emotional literacy tools across books
  • Predictable story pacing to soothe overstimulation
  • Simple metaphors that toddlers can feel in their bodies
  • Sibling connection as a model for co-regulation
  • Gentle, magical realism to create emotional safety
