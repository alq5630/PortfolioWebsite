About me

I am an educator, curriculum designer, and emerging software engineer with a background in health sciences, psychology, and empathy research. I have taught and supported students in community college massage therapy programs, designed continuing education style trainings for practitioners, and built home learning frameworks for early childhood.

My work often sits where disciplines meet. I like to translate complex concepts in neuroscience, trauma, and emotional development into language and structures that real people can use. I care deeply about how learning feels in the body and how it changes the way people move through their lives.

I am currently expanding my technical skills through formal software engineering study. This lets me think about learning experiences not only from the perspective of curriculum and facilitation, but also in terms of systems, interfaces, and data.

Areas of focus

  • Educational content design and curriculum mapping
  • Health sciences and massage therapy education
  • Psychology and empathy focused psychoeducation
  • Early childhood and parent facing learning supports
  • Learning experience design for adult professionals
  • Introductory computer science and coding friendly explanations

Current interests

Right now I am especially interested in how empathy, regulation, and cognition interact during learning, and how we can design educational materials that support both understanding and nervous system resilience. I am also exploring how code and automation can free up educators to spend more time in genuine human connection.
