Research and Framework Design

Empathy Spectrum Disorder Framework

The Empathy Spectrum Disorder framework is a model I developed to describe patterns of emotional processing, overresponsivity, underresponsivity, and strategic empathy across six core subtypes.

This project combines literature review, conceptual mapping, and clinical tool design. It includes a subtype model, clinician intake form, decision making algorithm, and visual spectrum map intended to support therapists, educators, and researchers in assessing and working with empathy related patterns.

My Role

Responsibilities

  • Mapped a six subtype empathy spectrum model based on existing research and lived experience patterns.
  • Drafted the ESDI Clinician Form to guide structured intake and subtype identification.
  • Created a subtype identification algorithm that walks clinicians through branching decisions.
  • Designed a visual empathy spectrum map to make the framework easy to understand at a glance.
  • Wrote a white paper and abstract that explain the conceptual foundations and clinical relevance.
  • Collaborated with editors and interviewers to share the framework through public facing media.

Focus Areas

  • Making a complex, emotionally charged topic feel structured and approachable.
  • Balancing theory with practical tools that clinicians can immediately apply.
  • Using visuals and decision trees to support different thinking styles.
  • Carefully chosen language that reduces stigma and centers regulation and safety.
