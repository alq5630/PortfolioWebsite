Process

How I Create Educational Experiences

My work combines curriculum design, emotional development, instructional psychology, and creative storytelling. Every project begins with understanding the learner and ends with material that is clear, accessible, and grounded. I design with intention, structure, and care so learners can move through content with confidence instead of overwhelm.

Phase 1: Discovery and Understanding

Before creating anything, I take time to understand who the learners are and what they need. This includes learning goals, emotional readiness, cultural context, and the skills they bring with them. I focus on the human side of learning so the final content feels natural and supportive.

  • Needs assessment and problem definition
  • Learner profiles and emotional considerations
  • Interviews and context gathering
  • Clear articulation of goals and outcomes
  • Accessibility and neurodiversity considerations

Guiding Questions

  • Who is the learner and what matters to them
  • Where might confusion or emotional difficulty arise
  • What knowledge or skills do they need to gain
  • How can the material feel safe, organized, and clear

Phase 2: Structure and Curriculum Design

Once I understand the learner and the desired outcomes, I begin building a strong structure. I break concepts into meaningful sections, create logical sequencing, and determine which formats will support the material best. This step shapes the entire learning journey.

  • Curriculum mapping and scaffolding
  • Learning objectives aligned with outcomes
  • Lesson flow and module architecture
  • Formats chosen for clarity and accessibility
  • Integration of emotional and cognitive considerations

Outputs

  • Full curriculum outline
  • Lesson and module structure
  • Storyboard for videos or visual media
  • Assessment plan that supports growth

Phase 3: Creation and Development

With the structure in place, I begin producing the content itself. I write, design, record, illustrate, and prototype depending on the needs of the project. My style is warm, grounded, and clear. I focus on reducing overwhelm and helping learners feel connected to the material.

  • Writing scripts, lessons, workbooks, and narrative content
  • Creating slides, handouts, illustrations, and multimedia
  • Developing interactive or web based components
  • Recording audio and video when needed
  • Ensuring emotional and cognitive approachability

Formats I Work With

  • Print based curriculum and workbooks
  • Digital downloads and handouts
  • Slide decks and visual lessons
  • Video scripts and short educational videos
  • Web and interactive content through HTML and CSS

Phase 4: Refinement and Testing

After creating the first version of the material, I refine it through feedback and testing. I check clarity, emotional tone, pacing, and usability. My goal is to make sure every piece of content feels supportive, intuitive, and effective.

  • User testing with learners or instructors
  • Assessment review and clarity adjustments
  • Tone, voice, and accessibility edits
  • Feedback loops with subject matter experts
  • Proofreading and quality control

Questions I Always Ask

  • Does this feel calm and understandable
  • Does the sequencing make sense for real learners
  • Is anything overwhelming or confusing
  • How do learners feel while using this material

Phase 5: Final Delivery and Support

Once the material is complete, I prepare it for delivery. This includes formatting, packaging, instructions, and any needed support for instructors, parents, or students. I want the final experience to feel polished, consistent, and ready for real use.

  • Print ready formatting and layout
  • Digital packaging and cohesive file structure
  • Instructor guides and implementation notes
  • Technical support for digital resources
  • Long term adaptability and updates

Final Deliverables Might Include

  • Curriculum packets and handouts
  • Video lessons or audio guides
  • Interactive web components
  • Instructor manuals and training guides
  • Accessible, easy to navigate resource folders

I design content that feels grounded and clear

If you want educational resources that are structured, warm, and emotionally aware, I would be happy to help.
