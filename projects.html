Empathy Spectrum Disorder (ESD) Clinician Training Module

Format: Online training module • Role: Sole designer and author

Overview

This project introduces the Empathy Spectrum Disorder framework to clinicians and advanced students. It includes a slide deck, facilitator notes, a learner workbook, a self inventory, and a decision flow chart that helps participants differentiate between hyperempathy, hypoempathy, dysregulation, shutdown, cognitive dominant patterns, and dark empathy.

My contributions

  • Defined learning objectives and module outcomes
  • Structured content into digestible lessons with reflection points
  • Wrote all psychoeducational explanations in accessible language
  • Created self inventory items and scoring guidance
  • Designed a visual flow chart for differential insight

  • Slide deck excerpt (PDF)
  • Learner workbook sample (PDF)
  • Self inventory pages (PDF)

I can provide full materials upon request to protect in progress publication.

Design highlights

  • Uses case vignettes that invite curiosity rather than quick diagnoses
  • Balances conceptual depth with concrete examples and scripts
  • Includes self reflective prompts for clinicians to map their own patterns
  • Integrates both affective and cognitive empathy dimensions
  • Built to function in live workshops or asynchronous settings

Massage Therapy Anatomy and Physiology Support Kit

Format: Printable and digital resources • Role: Instructor and designer

Overview

A suite of resources designed for community college massage therapy students to support mastery of anatomy, physiology, and clinical reasoning. Materials include fill in the blank worksheets, clinical flow cards, origin insertion action drills, and muscle meditation scripts that link textbook content to lived body awareness.

My contributions

  • Mapped objectives to state board and licensing exam content
  • Created progressive practice tasks for different learning styles
  • Designed clinical scenarios that connect theory to hands on work
  • Built simple tracking tools for students to monitor their progress

Samples

  • Worksheet excerpt (PDF)
  • Clinical flow checklist (PDF)
  • Muscle meditation script (PDF)

Design highlights

  • Integrates kinesthetic, visual, and verbal learning paths
  • Helps learners see patterns across muscles rather than memorize in isolation
  • Supports anxious test takers with clear structure and repetition
  • Aligns with chapter readings while staying flexible for different instructors

Sound and Silence: Year One Home Learning Framework

Format: Parent facing curriculum • Role: Creator and writer

Overview

A gentle home learning framework for the first year of structured exploration. The curriculum is organized around sound, silence, rhythm, and sensory discovery. It guides caregivers through simple weekly themes and activities that nurture language, motor development, and emotional regulation without forcing rigid milestones.

My contributions

  • Defined an overall arc for the year with seasonal and developmental anchors
  • Designed weekly activity menus instead of fixed daily scripts
  • Wrote caregiver reflection prompts and observation notes
  • Incorporated early exposure to multiple languages and musicality

Samples

  • Month overview page (PDF)
  • Weekly theme sample (PDF)
  • Caregiver observation log (PDF)

Design highlights

  • Designed to reduce pressure on caregivers while still feeling intentional
  • Aligns with Montessori inspired and child led approaches
  • Uses simple materials and realistic time blocks
  • Centers connection and curiosity more than performance
