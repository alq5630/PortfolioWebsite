The ESD Method™ — 12-Hour CE Training

Format: 12-hour NCBTMB-eligible training • Role: Course creator, instructional designer, writer, researcher
Audience: Licensed massage therapists, bodyworkers, and somatic practitioners

Overview

The ESD Method™ is a somatically grounded, trauma-informed training designed to help massage and bodywork practitioners recognize empathy dysregulation, adapt touch pacing, and maintain emotional safety during treatment sessions. The training introduces the six Empathy Spectrum Disorder (ESD) subtypes, the ESD Treatment Triad, and practical tools for responding to hyperempathy, hypoempathy, dissociation, oscillation, and strategic cognitive empathy.

The course integrates polyvagal theory, somatic tracking, boundaries work, and subtype-specific touch strategies. Its emphasis is on scope-appropriate client care, nervous-system regulation, and practitioner self-awareness.

What Practitioners Learn

  • How empathy dysregulation develops across the lifespan
  • Subtype-specific behavioral, somatic, and energetic markers
  • How to identify a client’s ESD subtype in the first 2–3 minutes
  • The relationship between trauma, touch, and emotional overwhelm
  • How to stabilize clients who flood, shut down, oscillate, or detach
  • How to apply the ESD Treatment Triad
  • Touch pacing modifications for each subtype
  • Subtype-specific communication and boundary strategies
  • How to assess their own empathy subtype and its clinical impact
  • How to maintain scope, safety, and emotional integrity

Course Modules (12 Hours)

Module objectives source: :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Module 1 — The Empathy Spectrum (2 hours)

Defines affective, cognitive, somatic, and energetic empathy; introduces the six ESD subtypes and their markers; includes guided self-inventory.

Module 2 — Trauma, Touch, and Empathy (2 hours)

Explores polyvagal theory, trauma-informed care, and nervous system cues of overwhelm, shutdown, or dissociation.

Module 3 — The ESD Treatment Framework (2 hours)

Teaches the ESD Treatment Triad: Somatic Regulation, Empathic Containment, and Attuned Boundaries.

Module 4 — Touch Techniques by Subtype (2 hours)

Demonstrates pacing, pressure, and rhythm adjustments for each subtype.

Module 5 — Communication & Boundaries (2 hours)

Subtype-specific scripts, countertransference awareness, and ethical boundaries.

Module 6 — The ESD Session Flow (2 hours)

Covers full session mapping: intake → pacing → mid-session safety → subtype-informed closing → aftercare.

Practitioner Self-Inventory

Practitioners complete a detailed self-inventory to identify their own empathy subtype patterns, activation tendencies, and influences on touch and communication. The assessment covers affective sensitivity, cognitive empathy, boundaries, relational dynamics, and energetic attunement.

Quick Reference: Subtype Touch Guide

A concise clinical handout provides subtype-specific touch pacing, somatic cues, communication strategies, red flags, and aftercare suggestions. This enables rapid adaptation during real sessions.

The ESD Session Flow Chart

This visual map guides therapists step-by-step through intake, subtype identification, treatment pacing, red flag detection, and subtype-specific aftercare. Each branch reflects real emotional and somatic patterns common in massage therapy.

Assessment Method

The course concludes with a written assessment including multiple-choice and short-answer questions. Practitioners must demonstrate understanding of subtype identification, somatic regulation, boundaries, and scope-informed decision-making.

Assessment details source: :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

  • 15-question quiz (12 MCQ + 3 short answers)
  • Passing score: 80%
  • Open notes allowed
  • Administered at the end of Module 6
  • Aligns with NCBTMB CE standards
