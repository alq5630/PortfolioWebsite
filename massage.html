Massage Therapy Curriculum & Instructional Resource Suite

Format: Multi modal curriculum • Role: Instructor, curriculum designer, media creator
Audience: Community college massage therapy students (first year through pre licensing)

Overview

This curriculum was created for community college massage therapy students who need to build a solid foundation in anatomy, physiology, clinical reasoning, and professional touch. Many students enter the program with uneven science backgrounds, strong hands on instincts, and significant test anxiety. I wanted to create a learning environment that felt structured, clear, accessible, and deeply embodied.

The full curriculum integrates a complete course website, PowerPoint lecture decks, quizzes and assessments, printable worksheets, clinical flow checklists, guided muscle meditations, demonstration videos, and chapter aligned study resources. Each piece is designed to reduce overwhelm, support different learning styles, and help students connect textbook content to the lived experience of their bodies and the bodies they work with.

Goals of the curriculum

  • Make anatomy and physiology feel approachable and pattern based, not like memorizing random lists
  • Support students with test anxiety through scaffolding, predictable structure, and repetition
  • Encourage hands on exploration as they learn origins, insertions, and actions
  • Build clinical reasoning, not just memorization
  • Increase confidence for both classroom exams and licensing preparation
  • Make each lesson feel organized, grounded, and applicable to real massage practice

Components I created

1. Full course website

I built a central course website that serves as the home base for students. It houses weekly modules, chapter resources, lecture notes, study guides, and lab preparation. Content is organized in a predictable way so that overwhelmed or neurodivergent learners can quickly see what is due, what to review, and how pieces connect. The site is mobile friendly so students can review materials on the go between work, family responsibilities, and class.

2. PowerPoint lecture decks

For key chapters, I designed visual, digestible slide decks that walk students from concept to example to clinical application. Slides use simplified diagrams, clear labeling, and consistent visual language to reduce cognitive load. Each deck is structured to support notetaking and later review rather than overwhelming students with text.

3. Quizzes and assessments

I created low stakes chapter quizzes, origin insertion action recall drills, applied scenario questions, and short answer reflections. These assessments are aligned with course outcomes and licensing expectations, and they give both students and instructors real information about what is landing and what needs more support.

4. Muscle meditations

Muscle meditations are a unique tool I developed to help students learn anatomy through guided breath, body awareness, and movement cues. Instead of memorizing a list of facts, students are invited to feel specific muscles, notice how they move, and connect that experience to origin, insertion, and action. These scripts also help anxious learners ground themselves before exams or hands on sessions.

5. Printable workbook pages

I designed chapter aligned worksheets, origin insertion action practice sheets, review pages, and weekly progress trackers that students can use in lab, clinic, or self study. The pages are simple, repeatable, and focused on helping students see patterns across content rather than cramming the night before an exam.

6. Clinical flow checklists and videos

To support integration, I created clinical flow checklists that guide students through intake, assessment, planning, technique, and closing. I also planned and began producing short demonstration videos on palpation landmarks, body mechanics, and stroke sequences to reinforce what we cover in person.

My contributions

  • Designed the overall curriculum architecture across lecture, lab, and self study
  • Built and maintained the course website and weekly module structure
  • Wrote and designed lecture slides for key anatomy and physiology chapters
  • Developed written materials including worksheets, checklists, and study guides
  • Created assessment questions aligned with program and licensing outcomes
  • Authored muscle meditation scripts and integrated them into class flow
  • Designed clinical reasoning worksheets based on real client scenarios
  • Integrated evidence informed pedagogy and trauma aware teaching practices
  • Gathered student feedback and iterated on materials during live use

Design highlights

  • Clear scaffolding so students always know what to focus on each week
  • Repetition with variation to strengthen memory without becoming monotonous
  • Multiple learning modalities for visual, somatic, written, and auditory learners
  • Anxiety friendly structure with predictable pacing and low stakes check ins
  • Real world application woven into every chapter and practice session
  • Embodied learning through guided meditations and movement based exploration
  • Alignment with licensing exam content for long term relevance

Future development

Next steps for this curriculum include expanding the workbook into a fully illustrated student manual, filming the complete demonstration video library, converting the muscle meditations into an audio playlist, and exploring digital flashcard and app based versions of the study tools. The existing suite already functions well in a community college setting and can be scaled into continuing education courses for new massage therapists and instructors.
